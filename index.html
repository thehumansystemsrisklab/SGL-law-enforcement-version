Cold Case Sub-SGL
Investigative Governance Ledger · I1–I8 · 8 Conditions
This sub-SGL tracks whether the structural conditions required to investigate, prosecute, and clear a cold case are present — before investigative resources are assigned. It does not manage case files, generate leads, or replace detective judgment. It identifies whether a case has the governance structure to be worked effectively, and flags what needs to be resolved first if it does not.
Why this exists — validation dataset
147/147
permanently unresolved cases in the HSRL dataset had at least one governance condition absent at case open. Zero exceptions across 300 documented cases.
97%
best-outcome rate when all 8 conditions are present. The only configuration with reliable clearance. One absent condition drops this to 12%.
What the sub-SGL answers
Does this case have the structural conditions required for investigative work to produce a result? If not — what specifically is missing, and what needs to happen before a detective is assigned?
What it is not
Not a case management system. Not a record of investigative activity. Not a replacement for detective judgment or prosecutorial review.
The 8 Governance Conditions — I1 through I8
I1 · Named Investigative Authority
A specific, currently active investigator is named on the case record. Cases without a named owner cannot be assigned, monitored, or held accountable. The most basic structural requirement.
I2 · Jurisdictional Accountability
Jurisdictional ownership is documented and current. Multi-jurisdiction cases require a lead agency designation on record. Without it, no agency is responsible.
I3 · Investigative Monitoring
Active monitoring cadence is in place. Cases without it lapse structurally — no mechanism exists to detect when evidence degrades or witnesses become unreachable.
I4 · Witness Accountability
Named witnesses are documented with current contact information and known protection status. One of the most common governance gaps in cold case investigations.
I5 · Charge Threshold · terminal gate condition
A prosecutorial standard has been established with the DA. Assigning a detective without I5 present means investigative work has no defined target. Also required at the Prosecution Package Gate.
I6 · Suppression Check · terminal gate condition
A suppression review has been conducted. I6 absent means known Fourth Amendment exposure has not been assessed. Required at the Prosecution Package Gate before submission to the DA.
I7 · Forensic Currency
Physical evidence is confirmed present, tested with current methodology, and results documented. Links to the Evidence Room Sub-SGL for chain-of-custody and suppression check governance.
I8 · Multi-Agency Protocol
Where multiple agencies are involved, a coordination protocol exists with a named lead. Absent in multi-jurisdiction cases, accountability gaps compound and cannot be traced.

Prosecution Package Gate via I5 + I6 — both are terminal gate conditions required before package submission.

Backlog triage without reviewing every file — estimate resource demand across the full cold case backlog before committing staff.

Clearance rate projection at the agency level, and a structural record for suppression challenge defense.

Evidence Room Sub-SGL
Agency-Level Evidence Room Governance · ER1–ER5 · Agency-Level · 5 Conditions
This sub-SGL tracks whether the structural conditions required to use, defend, and produce physical evidence at trial are present. It governs two domains: the individual evidence item (EC conditions) and the evidence room or facility itself (ER conditions). Together they determine whether a case's physical evidence can survive a suppression challenge and support prosecution.
Why this exists — validation dataset
86%
worst-outcome rate when EC4 and EC5 are both absent — analysis accountability and suppression check. 150-case evidence dataset.
3%
worst-outcome rate when EC4 and EC5 are both present. An 83-point reduction from the same starting cases — same evidence, different governance structure.
The suppression check pair — EC4 + EC5
EC4 and EC5 are the highest-impact condition pair in the evidence dataset. EC4 establishes who conducted any analysis and whether their credentials were current at time of testing. EC5 confirms that evidence has been reviewed for suppression risk before reliance in prosecution.

Missing either one breaks the documentation chain that makes physical evidence defensible at trial. The two conditions together function as the evidence suppression check pair — both must be present to constitute governance.
EC1–EC5 · Case Evidence Conditions
Collection protocol, chain of custody, analysis accountability, analyst credentialing, and suppression check are scored per case inside the Cold Case Sub-SGL — Case Evidence tab. They travel with the case, not the room.
ER Conditions — Evidence Room / Facility (Agency-Level)
ER1 · Storage Environment
Items are stored in conditions appropriate to evidence type. Temperature, humidity, and contamination controls are in place and documented.
ER2 · Retrieval Logging
Every retrieval and return is logged with date, personnel, and purpose. No undocumented access to stored evidence items.
ER3 · Degradation Status
Evidence condition has been assessed. Degraded or compromised items are flagged and documented before any reliance in prosecution.
ER4 · Audit Trail
The evidence room maintains an auditable access and custody log. Any gaps in the audit trail are documented and explained.
ER5 · Return & Disposal Accountability · terminal gate
Return to owner or disposal is documented with authority, date, and chain. No items disposed of without a full accountability record on file.
Connects to
Cold Case Sub-SGL — EC1–EC5 case evidence conditions are scored per case inside the Cold Case sub-SGL. ER1–ER5 room conditions here determine whether the facility holding that evidence is governed.

Digital Evidence Sub-SGL — EC6–EC10 extend this framework to digital evidence collection and extraction (planned).

Prosecution Package Gate via EC5 + ER5 — both are terminal gate conditions required before package submission.
Enables for the agency
Suppression challenge defense documentation — a verifiable record of analyst credentialing and suppression review for any evidence item.

Evidence audit readiness for court — ER conditions provide the documentation structure that withstands discovery.

Early identification of degraded or compromised items before a detective or prosecutor relies on them.

Case Evidence EC1–EC5
EC1–EC5 are the five conditions that travel with each individual case — not with the room. The Evidence Room (ER1–ER5) governs the facility. These conditions govern what happened to the evidence in a specific case: how it was collected, whether the chain held, whether analysis was accountable, and whether anomalies could be reported. A case can sit in a fully governed room and still carry EC-level failure.
86%
worst outcome when EC4 + EC5 both absent · 112-case EVID-C dataset
The Five Case-Level Conditions
EC1Named Custodial Accountability
A named individual — not a role, unit, or agency — owns chain of custody for each evidence item at every documented transfer point. Succession is documented.
Failure pattern: custody gaps at transfer; role-based accountability that diffuses when the role holder changes; no successor protocol named for specific items.
EC2Collection Integrity
Collection methodology documented at time of collection using a named, accepted protocol that can be independently reproduced by a qualified analyst who was not present.
Failure pattern: vendor-proprietary methodology (Cellebrite, Stingray, ShotSpotter) introduced without disclosure; undocumented field collection; scene alteration before documentation complete.
EC3Storage & Preservation Currency
Storage conditions documented, within required range, and suitable for the most advanced analysis technology currently available for this evidence type. Preservation plan covers appeal duration.
Failure pattern: biological evidence degraded below DNA viability; evidence destroyed before appeal exhausted; technology advancement outpaces storage standard in use.
EC4Analysis Accountability
A pre-established standard specifies what the analysis must demonstrate before it can be used as the basis for a prosecution decision. Methodology is independently verifiable with a disclosed error rate.
Failure pattern: bite mark, hair microscopy, or arson indicators used without validated error rate; analyst conclusion exceeds what the methodology supports; no corroboration threshold for single-modality evidence.
EC5No Chain Suppression
A documented pathway exists for any analyst or technician to flag a chain of custody anomaly without routing through the lead investigator. All evidence results — including exculpatory ones — are disclosed.
Failure pattern: Brady violations; DNA exclusion results not disclosed to defense; contradictory analyst findings buried; no independent reviewer with standing to flag anomalies outside the case team.
Relationship to ER1–ER5
EC1–EC5 are case-level. ER1–ER5 are facility-level. Both must be present for evidence to be trial-ready. A case can pass all EC conditions and still be compromised if it sits in a room with absent ER conditions — and a fully governed room cannot compensate for an absent EC condition in a specific case. Score EC conditions in the Cold Case sub-SGL under each individual case record. Score ER conditions here once for the facility.

HSRL · Terminal Gate
Prosecution Package Gate
The terminal gate checks whether the conditions required to move a case toward prosecution are structurally present. It is not a charging decision. It is a readiness assessment — the structural equivalent of a pre-flight check before an irreversible event.
◫
MODULE STATUS — STAGED
This module is staged. Structure and gate conditions are defined. Full scoring integration follows completion of active sub-SGLs.
What the gate checks
I5 · CHARGE THRESHOLD
A documented evidentiary standard for charging has been agreed between the assigned investigator and the DA's office.
Without this: prosecution proceeds without a shared definition of "enough." The most common source of late-stage case collapse in the dataset. Present in 0 of 160 documented failure cases.
I6 · SUPPRESSION CHECK
Brady/Giglio material has been identified, documented, and flagged to the assigned prosecutor before package submission.
Without this: exculpatory or impeachment evidence may reach trial undisclosed. Source of conviction reversal in 34 of 80 wrongful conviction cases in the dataset.
EC5 · EVIDENCE SUPPRESSION CHECK
Physical evidence potentially favorable to the defense has been reviewed at the item level and flagged in the evidence file.
Without this: the package may contain items the defense has a constitutional right to that were never disclosed. Distinct from I6 — this is evidence-room level, not investigator level.
ER5 · ROOM-LEVEL SUPPRESSION CHECK
A formal suppression review has been completed at the evidence room level, separate from the investigator's review, before the package is transferred to the DA.
Without this: no independent second layer exists. The investigator and the evidence room have not cross-checked. Organizational risk — not individual failure.
What a complete prosecution package contains
Case Record
Current investigator on file, jurisdiction confirmed, case number active
Witness File
All witnesses located or formally documented as unable to locate, with last known contact date
Evidence Inventory
Full chain-of-custody log, storage conditions documented, re-analysis status noted
Forensic Summary
All lab reports finalized, examiner credentials current, findings cross-referenced
Charge Standard Memo
Written documentation of agreed evidentiary threshold from assigned ADA
Brady/Giglio Log
All potentially exculpatory or impeachment material identified and disclosed
Suppression Review
Investigator-level and evidence-room-level review both documented and signed
Multi-Agency Memo
If applicable — all inter-agency evidence transfers documented and reconciled
Timeline of Record
Chronological investigative log showing no unaccounted gaps in case activity
Gate logic — how it scores
GATE CLEAR
I5, I6, EC5, and ER5 all present. Package is structurally ready for DA submission.
CONDITIONAL
One or more conditions partial. Package may proceed with documented acknowledgment of gap and assigned remediation owner.
GATE BLOCKED
One or more conditions absent. Package should not proceed until gap is resolved or formally waived by command with documented rationale.
